Your visual programming workspace. Drag and drop command blocks to build complex workflows without writing a single line of code.

🕹️ Manual Drive Mode

Take manual control of your creation. The dashboard automatically builds a virtual vertical joystick for every motor you connect.

  • Slide up or down to rotate cogs and move joints
  • Drive wheels and steering mechanisms instantly
  • Release to automatically stop

Watch your robot map its own body! In this mode, the phone camera watches your creation and helps it discover its kinematics.

  • Background Calibration & Static differencing

Turn your stepper motor creation into an autonomous self-driving car. Maps the room using your phone's camera!

  • Dynamic 2D map overlay on live video
  • Maps safe paths (Green) and obstacles (Red)

⚡ The Zero-Component "Minimum Build"

No fancy capacitors, no protoboards, no complicated tools. This is the absolute simplest way to build the robot. It's so easy, a 12-year-old can put it together in an afternoon!

🛒 1. Order the Core Parts

Go to AliExpress and search for:
• 1x ESP32-S2 Mini (~$2.00)
• 2x 28BYJ-48 Stepper Motors (Make sure they come with the ULN2003 Driver Boards! ~$3.00)
• 1x Pack of Female-to-Female Dupont Wires (~$1.00)

🧱 2. Print the LEGO® Adapters

While your parts are shipping, use a 3D printer (at home, school, or a local library) to print the Brikkitron Enclosures. These let you snap your motors directly into standard LEGO® Technic pieces so you don't have to build a chassis from scratch!

🔥 3. Solder the Power (The Only Hard Part!)

Take two Dupont jumper wires, strip the ends, and solder them directly to the 5V and GND holes on the ESP32-S2 Mini. Then, plug the other ends into the + and - power pins on both of your ULN2003 driver boards to share the power.

🔌 4. Plug It All In!

• Plug Female-to-Female wires from ESP32 pins 1, 2, 3, 4 into Driver Board A.
• Plug wires from ESP32 pins 5, 7, 9, 11 into Driver Board B.
• Plug the white motor cables into the driver boards.
• Plug a standard USB Phone Power Bank into the ESP32. You're done!

Pro-Tip: Because this simple version skips the "smoothing capacitor", running both motors at absolute maximum speed simultaneously might occasionally cause the robot to reboot. But it works perfectly for 90% of beginner projects!
